# 48hrs --- AI Travel Planner

## Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## 1. Product Overview

**Product Name:** 48hrs (working title)\
**Product Type:** Mobile-first AI travel planning app

### Core Value Proposition

For time-starved professionals planning short 1--4 day trips, 48hrs
generates a personalized, trustworthy 48-hour itinerary in under 15
minutes. This reduces planning time from \~16 hours of research to
minutes while increasing confidence in choices.

The itinerary should feel: - Curated - Local - Context-aware -
Energy-balanced

It must never feel generic or algorithmic.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Target Users

## Primary Persona --- Time-Starved Professional

**Demographics** - Age: 28--50 - Busy professional - High disposable
income - Travels 3--6 short trips per year - Often books trips last
minute

**Psychographics** - Values experiences over tourist checklists - Wants
minimal planning effort - Prefers curated recommendations - High comfort
with mobile technology

**Core Motivations** - Save planning time - Reduce decision fatigue -
Feel confident in itinerary choices - Avoid missing good experiences

**Biggest Pain** Research overload and uncertainty about whether choices
are actually good.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Secondary Persona --- Overwhelmed Group Planner

One person usually plans the entire trip.

Needs: - Fast group preference collection - Low social friction - Clear
consensus signals

Pain points: - Budget differences - Energy differences - People not
responding

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Core Problems

### 1. Research Overload

Planning requires: - Google searches - Blog research - Maps
exploration - Social media inspiration - Restaurant hunting

This process can take 10--16 hours for a weekend trip.

### 2. Distrust in Generic Recommendations

Most trip planners produce: - Generic lists - Tourist-heavy options -
Poor personalization

Users often double-check recommendations manually.

### 3. Group Coordination Friction

When traveling with others: - Preferences conflict - Planning falls on
one person - Communication is chaotic

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. MVP Feature Scope

## Feature 1 --- Smart Intake → Instant Itinerary

### Description

A fast interactive quiz (5--6 questions) capturing travel preferences.

Completion target: ≤ 2 minutes

### Intake Questions

1.  Energy Level
2.  Budget Range (slider with estimated \$/day)
3.  Food Preferences
4.  Walking Tolerance
5.  Hidden Gems vs Tourist Spots
6.  Group Size
7.  Destination City

### Design Principles

-   Conversational interaction
-   Visual sliders
-   Estimated cost shown early
-   Prefilled taste profile for returning users

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Feature 2 --- Flexible Block Itinerary

Structure: Morning\
Afternoon\
Evening

Each block includes: - 1 primary recommendation - 1 backup option - Cost
estimate - Explanation for selection

### Example Block

Morning

Cafe Lumiere\
Local espresso bar + pastry shop

Why this for you:\
You chose hidden gems + relaxed mornings.

Backup Option:\
Farmers Market

Cost: \~\$12--18

### Design Principles

-   Proximity clustering
-   Energy-aware pacing
-   Optional rest block

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Feature 3 --- Intelligent Swap

Users regenerate only a single itinerary block.

Example: Tap Swap → Afternoon

System regenerates: - New activity - Nearby alternatives - Maintains
itinerary structure

Rules: - Preserve travel flow - Maintain vibe alignment - Maintain
proximity logic

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Feature 4 --- Lightweight Group Preference Sync

Organizer creates shareable intake link.

Each participant: - Completes a 2--3 minute intake

System: - Aggregates preferences - Shows trade-offs - Displays which
users preferences are satisfied

Organizer finalizes plan.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
